History of Angkor Wat

Angkor Wat, located in Cambodia, is the largest religious monument in the world and a masterpiece of Khmer architecture. Originally built in the 12th century by King Suryavarman II as a Hindu temple dedicated to Vishnu, it later transformed into a Buddhist site. The temple is renowned for its grand towers, intricate bas-reliefs, and extensive galleries depicting Hindu mythology and Khmer history.

Surrounded by a vast moat, Angkor Wat symbolizes Mount Meru, the center of the universe in Hindu and Buddhist beliefs. Today, it stands as Cambodia’s most iconic landmark, attracting millions of visitors and serving as a symbol of national pride.
